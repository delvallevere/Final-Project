Hydration & Diet

Drink plenty of water:
Staying hydrated is crucial for lubricating your vocal cords.
Avoid dehydrating drinks:
Limit or avoid caffeine, alcohol, and soda, as they can dry out your vocal cords
Limit irritants:
Avoid chocolate, dairy, and acidic foods like orange juice that can cause phlegm or acid reflux.
Eat well:
A nutritious diet can help stabilize blood sugar and support vocal function.

Rest & Recovery

Get enough sleep:
Aim for 8-10 hours of sleep the night before to allow your voice time to recover.
Rest your voice:
Get on "vocal rest" the day before, limiting talking, whispering, and shouting.
Avoid throat clearing:
Instead of clearing your throat with a "ahem," swallow or use a gentle, breathy cough to clear mucus.

Warming Up & Vocal Technique

Warm up your voice:
Start with gentle exercises like humming, lip trills, or gentle scales 10-20 minutes before performing to prepare your vocal cords.
Practice good posture:
Proper alignment helps support your voice.
Breathe correctly:
Learn and use proper breath support to avoid straining.
Avoid strain:
Do not shout, whisper excessively, or sing to your maximum capacity during warm-ups.
